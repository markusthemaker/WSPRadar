# Part 0 Review — Preface

## Decision

Retain the production Part 0 as the baseline. It now performs its intended function well:

1. begins with the real station-development measurement problem;
2. recognizes rapid live A/B switching as a strong existing amateur-radio method;
3. explains the remaining one-path, one-time and sequential limitations;
4. introduces WSPR as a scalable observational basis without calling it calibrated instrumentation;
5. positions WSPRadar as the experiment-selection, conditioning and audit layer;
6. distinguishes experiment designs and their valid claim boundaries;
7. introduces Success, Delta SNR and Decode Outcomes before Part I.

## Accepted change

### Replace causal-sounding map wording

Current wording in Section 0.3:

> The map locates the effect; it is the start of the analysis, not the conclusion.

Candidate wording:

> The map locates the observed pattern; it is the start of the analysis, not the conclusion.

Reason: the map localizes an observed result. It does not by itself establish a causal effect.

## No other structural change

- Keep the current title `0. Why WSPRadar?`.
- Keep Sections 0.0-0.3.
- Keep the conditional statement that hundreds to thousands of observations may accumulate depending on band, activity and time window.
- Keep the distinction between a semi-quantitative evidence base and calibrated laboratory measurement.
- Keep current source numbering during this review; Part 0 now establishes the global first-use order.
